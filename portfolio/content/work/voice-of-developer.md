## The goal

A platform with millions of developers hears from a tiny, unrepresentative fraction of them. Roadmaps built from the loudest enterprise voices systematically miss what the long tail needs — and the long tail is where platform growth compounds.

## What shipped

An AI-driven Voice of Developer program: a 360° pipeline that ingests unstructured developer signals at scale — Discord, Stack Overflow, GitHub issues, support tickets — and turns them into ranked, evidence-backed roadmap priorities. Combined with strategic customer input and org-wide dogfood events, it grounds product bets in what developers globally actually struggle with, not what the last escalation said.

It's a direct descendant of the Voice of Customer program I created at Mapbox — which spanned enterprise customers, SI partners, and scaled developers and directly produced five-plus new products — rebuilt for a platform ten times the size with AI doing the reading.

## Why it matters

Working backwards from growth starts with an honest picture of demand. This program is the demand-sensing layer: it finds the friction that's silently capping adoption, sizes it, and puts it in front of product and engineering with receipts.
