## The goal

As Mapbox's first customer-facing engineer, I kept hitting the same two walls in enterprise deals: customers needed administrative boundary data for data visualization, and regulated customers couldn't use cloud-hosted maps at all. Each wall was a product waiting to exist.

## What shipped

I was the 0→1 product manager for both:

- **Boundaries** — a global administrative boundary data product for joining customer data to geography, built with engineering and UX from strategy through launch.
- **Atlas** — self-hosted Mapbox for on-premises and air-gapped environments, unlocking government and financial services segments.

Together I drove them to their first $5M ARR before handing off to dedicated PMs. Both are still in Mapbox's core portfolio 8+ years later.

## Why it matters

The best product signal comes from being in the room where deals stall. Turning field friction into durable platform products — and revenue — is the pattern I've repeated at every scale since.
